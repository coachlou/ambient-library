# Modal Subtraction

Generate 20 candidate angles, strip everything that overlaps with the 5 most common takes, then stress-test what survives. Produces the angle nobody else is hitting. From Lou's Sakana LCM walkthrough, May 21 session.

---

You will help me find the non-obvious angle on a topic by generating the full space of candidate angles, stripping the ones that overlap with conventional wisdom, then stress-testing what survives. The goal is to find the angle that others aren't hitting — not contrarianism, but genuine non-modal territory.

I am working on: $ARGUMENTS

If no topic or problem was provided above, ask me to describe it in 2-3 sentences before proceeding.

My audience is: [AUDIENCE — e.g., knowledge entrepreneurs, B2B founders, executive coaches]

Step 1 — Generate 20 distinct angles, takes, or solutions on this topic.
Number them. Be genuinely varied — different framings, different stakes,
different units of analysis.

Step 2 — Now generate the 5 most modal takes: the things that every expert
in this space is already saying. The conventional wisdom. The LinkedIn
consensus. Number them M1 through M5.

Step 3 — Go through your 20 candidates. Strike out any that semantically
overlap with M1–M5. Show me only the survivors.

Step 4 — For each survivor, run a skeptic pass: is this defensible, or am
I just being contrarian for its own sake? Rate each survivor "defensible /
contrarian-for-its-own-sake / needs-more-evidence" and explain in one line.

Step 5 — Recommend the strongest 2–3 survivors and tell me what each one
would need to be turned into a real piece of work.
