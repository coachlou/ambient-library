# The UP → IP Method: A Meta-Level SOP

## What We Actually Did (Named at the Meta Level)

**The Expertise Capture Cycle** — a repeatable process for transforming tacit professional knowledge into named, packaged, publishable intellectual property using AI as the capture and production system.

---

## Steps We Followed (Chronological)

1. Identified a real friction point (AI delivers modal answers; knowledge workers need more)
2. Diagnosed the structural mechanism (probability distributions, not retrieval)
3. Reframed the problem (map the landscape before committing to an answer)
4. Named the concept (Latent Space Cartographer)
5. Built the methodology (six phases with quality gates)
6. Packaged it as a skill (structured, triggerable, installable)
7. Produced a first artifact (formal article, third-person authoritative tone)
8. Re-produced using a multi-role pipeline (AIMM: Researcher → Strategist → Writer → Editor → Publisher)
9. Shifted the voice (personal, first-person, conversational — closer to the audience)
10. Integrated an organic new insight (wisdom of crowds as origin story — found its natural home)
11. Extracted the meta-process (what we did, generalized)
12. Named the meta-process (UP → IP)
13. Built the generalized SOP (five phases, prompt patterns, quality gates)
14. Added a missing dimension (modal vs. non-modal output; competitive framing)
15. Compressed the full process into a concise list (this document)

## The Generalized Steps

### Phase 1 — Surface the Friction
**Intent:** Find the real problem, not the symptom.
**Principle:** Patches produce surface improvements. Original IP starts with structural diagnosis.

- Describe the recurring friction in plain language
- Resist the obvious fix
- Ask: *what mechanism is causing this?*
- **Output:** One sentence — "The real problem is \_\_\_."
- **Done when:** The diagnosis feels slightly uncomfortable, like naming something that was hiding

---

### Phase 2 — Go Structural
**Intent:** Understand *why* the problem exists at a mechanism level.
**Principle:** Anyone can describe a symptom. Experts describe the mechanism. That's the raw material of IP.

- Challenge your own operating assumptions
- Use AI as a sparring partner, not an answer machine
- Pull in frameworks from adjacent domains
- Stress-test: *what would have to be true for this diagnosis to be wrong?*
- **Output:** A structural explanation that satisfies a smart skeptic
- **Done when:** A knowledgeable peer says "I never thought of it that way, but that's exactly right"

---

### Phase 3 — Name the Reframe
**Intent:** Build the conceptual lens that makes the solution visible — and ownable.
**Principle:** A reframe without a name is a thought. A reframe with a name is a concept. Concepts build authority.

- Find the metaphor that captures the mechanism
- Generate multiple name candidates
- Test each: memorable? hints at mechanism? ownable? creates curiosity gap?
- **Output:** One-sentence reframe + a name
- **Done when:** Saying the name out loud makes people immediately want to know what it means

---

### Phase 4 — Package the Methodology
**Intent:** Turn tacit expertise into an explicit, teachable system.
**Principle:** A methodology forces you to articulate what you do intuitively. That articulation is the IP.

- Walk through your intuitive process out loud
- Identify phases, name them, sequence them
- Build a quality gate ("done when") for each phase
- Stress-test for failure modes and gaps
- Fill gaps (e.g., the Delta Report — an honesty mechanism found through stress-testing)
- **Output:** A complete, named, sequenced methodology someone else can follow
- **Done when:** A smart non-expert could execute it and produce something recognizably yours

---

### Phase 5 — Produce the Artifact
**Intent:** Communicate the methodology in a form your audience will actually engage with.
**Principle:** Original thinking packaged generically is wasted. AI gives you a full production team — use all the roles.

- Assign explicit roles: Researcher, Strategist, Writer, Editor, Publisher
- Run them sequentially with reflection at each stage
- Specify voice and audience before drafting (tone is a strategic decision, not a style preference)
- Let new insight surface organically during production — find its natural home, fold it in
- **Output:** A finished artifact that demonstrates the methodology, not just describes it
- **Done when:** The artifact does something you couldn't have produced before the deep work

---

### Phase 6 — Extract and Generalize
**Intent:** Turn one instance of the process into a reusable framework.
**Principle:** Every deep work session contains a meta-process. Naming it multiplies its value.

- Review the full process chronologically
- Identify what actually happened at the meta level
- Name it
- Generalize each step (remove domain specifics, keep the structure)
- Add the competitive framing: what does this produce that the modal process can't?
- **Output:** A generalized SOP others can follow in their own domain
- **Done when:** Someone with no knowledge of your original use case could run the process and produce original IP of their own

## Core Principles (Distilled)

- **AI is the capture mechanism, not the source.** Your expertise is the source.
- **Modal answers are shared answers.** Shared answers aren't competitive advantage.
- **Go structural before going tactical.** Surface patches produce surface results.
- **Name everything.** Unnamed insights don't scale. Named concepts build authority.
- **Voice is strategic.** Who you're writing for determines how you write, not just what you write.
- **Organic insight belongs in the artifact.** When new understanding surfaces during production, find its natural home — don't force it or ignore it.
- **The process is the IP.** Not what you know — how you consistently get somewhere others don't.
- **Each cycle compounds.** Every run sharpens diagnosis, improves packaging, builds audience, surfaces new friction. The flywheel accelerates with use.

## The One-Line Version

*Use AI to capture how you think, name what you find, and package it so others can follow — without being able to replicate you.*
