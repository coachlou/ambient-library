# Writing Type: Personal Connection / Story Article

## What it is
A piece built around the writer's lived experience — a moment, a turning point, a thing they got wrong or finally got right. The story carries the piece, with the lesson surfacing naturally rather than being preached. Vulnerability is the credibility.

## Default length
600–1,200 words.

## Structure
- Opens in a specific scene — concrete details, sensory specifics
- Tells the story without rushing to the lesson
- Surfaces the insight gradually, ideally through what the writer did or felt, not what they "learned"
- Ends with a small turn — the reader sees the scene differently than they did at the start

## Voice
First person throughout. Specific sensory details. Comfortable with emotion, but earned, not performative. The writer is allowed to be unsure, wrong, or human. Avoid "and that's when I realized…" type lessons.

## What success looks like
The reader sees themselves in the story even though it's about someone else. They feel something specific, not just impressed. They remember the scene a week later.

## Watch out for
- Story that exists only to set up a lesson
- Generalizing in the middle of a specific scene
- Inspirational summary at the end ("And so I learned that…")
- Vulnerability that feels strategic rather than honest
- Names and details swapped out so heavily the story stops feeling real

## How roles use this file
- **Strategist:** the "thesis" is what the story reveals, not a stated claim — frame it as a question or a tension, not an argument
- **Researcher:** light touch — verify any factual references but the load is on the lived experience, not citations
- **Outliner:** often 3 movements: scene → complication → small turn
- **Drafter:** specificity over generalization; trust the reader to extract the lesson; show, don't summarize
- **Skeptic:** fact-check less aggressively — this is about emotional truth, not citation density
- **Line Editor:** cut any sentence that explains what the story already showed
