# Writing Type: How-To Tutorial

## What it is
A piece that promises the reader they will be able to do something specific by the end, and then delivers on that promise. The thesis is the promise. Clarity matters more than voice. Reader-first, ego-second.

## Default length
800–1,500 words.

## Structure
- Opens with the promise: what the reader will be able to do
- Names who this is for and who it's not for
- Walks through the steps or the framework with examples
- Calls out the common mistake or trap
- Ends with the reader's next action — concrete, doable today

## Voice
Friendly and direct. Second person ("you") throughout. Clear and patient. The writer's personality is welcome but should never get in the way of the instruction. Less rhetorical, more practical.

## What success looks like
The reader actually does the thing. They get the result. They come back to the piece as a reference. They send it to someone else with "this is exactly what you need."

## Watch out for
- Steps that assume knowledge the reader doesn't have
- Framework-for-the-sake-of-framework (3 letters that don't earn the abstraction)
- Hedging ("you might want to consider…") instead of clear instructions
- Overpromising in the opening, underdelivering in the body
- Personality interruptions that break the reader's flow

## How roles use this file
- **Strategist:** the thesis is the promise — be specific and achievable; "you will be able to X by the end" is the test
- **Researcher:** verify any claims about results, tools, examples, or recommended approaches
- **Outliner:** linear structure works best; numbered steps welcome; one worked example is worth three abstract ones
- **Drafter:** clarity over voice; use "you" throughout; show one worked example end-to-end
- **Skeptic:** verify every claim about outcomes, tools, or "this works because"
- **Line Editor:** if a sentence isn't helping the reader DO the thing, cut it
