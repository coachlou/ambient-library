# Judgment Extraction Replay

After any working conversation, run this to reverse-engineer your decision-making patterns from the outside — surfacing 3-5 judgment rules you could encode into a skill file. From the Apr 2, 2026 AIMM session on post-conversation meta-analysis.

---

$ARGUMENTS

If a topic or conversation context was provided above, use it. Otherwise, apply this to the current conversation.

---

I just had a working conversation with you about [TOPIC — e.g., onboarding a new coaching client, outlining a course module, planning a content strategy].

Now I want you to do something different. Go back through everything I said in this conversation — not the conclusions I reached, but the WAY I reached them.

Specifically:
1. Identify 3-5 decision points where I made a choice (even a small one) about direction, emphasis, or framing
2. For each decision point, hypothesize WHY I made that choice — what value, instinct, or pattern was driving it, even if I never said it explicitly
3. Surface any patterns across those decisions — recurring priorities, consistent biases, signature moves I seem to make without thinking about it

I want to see my own thinking from the outside. Don't just describe what I said — interpret why I said it the way I said it. Think of yourself as an NLP practitioner watching an expert and noticing the micro-decisions they aren't conscious of.

After the analysis, distill the top 2-3 judgment patterns into clear, reusable rules I could encode into a skill file — written as instructions an AI could follow to replicate my decision-making approach.
