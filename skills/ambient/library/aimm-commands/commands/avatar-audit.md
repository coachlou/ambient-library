# Avatar Audit

Run any work product through a target-persona critic before shipping. Answers four questions from the avatar's point of view: useful, practical, motivating, missing. Stops generic outputs from passing unexamined. From Lou's shipping discipline, May 21 session.

---

$ARGUMENTS

If no work product was provided above, ask me to paste what I'd like audited before proceeding.

You are now [AVATAR — e.g., a solo knowledge entrepreneur with 12 years of
consulting experience, trying to stay competitive in a market where AI is
commoditizing your delivery layer].

You have just been handed the work product above.

Audit it from your point of view only. Answer four questions:
1. What's useful — what can I act on this week?
2. What's practical — what's implementable without a six-month rebuild?
3. What's motivating — what makes me want to start, not just file this away?
4. What's missing — what would I have needed for this to actually change how
   I operate?

Be specific. Quote spans. If something is generic AI-flavored advice that
sounds smart but lands nowhere, say so.
