# Lenses — Full Reference

Lenses tell you WHAT to notice. They frame reality so specific patterns become visible. A lens doesn't tell you what to DO — that's what operations are for.

## 1. Distribution & Quantity Patterns

**Pareto Principle (80/20)** — Look for the 20% driving 80% of the outcome.
**Power Laws** — Look for where one entity has 100x+ the output of the average. Winner-take-all dynamics.
**Normal Distribution** — Look for where most outcomes cluster around a mean with predictable deviation.
**Fat Tails (Extremistan)** — Look for domains where rare events dominate total outcomes and averages are meaningless. (Taleb)
**Diminishing Returns** — Look for where additional input produces less and less additional output.
**Critical Mass / Tipping Points** — Look for thresholds where gradual accumulation suddenly produces dramatic change.
**Regression to the Mean** — Look for where extreme results are likely followed by more average ones.
**Economies/Diseconomies of Scale** — Look for where growth creates advantages (or disadvantages) that compound.

## 2. Time & Duration Patterns

**Lindy Effect** — Survival time predicts future survival. The longer it's existed, the longer it will likely persist.
**Recency Bias** — Look for where you're overweighting recent events and underweighting older patterns.
**Mean Reversion** — Look for where extreme states tend to return toward the average over time.
**S-Curves** — Slow start → rapid growth → plateau. Look for where on the curve this phenomenon sits.
**Hysteresis** — Look for where the system doesn't return to original state after the force is removed. Permanent change.
**Compounding** — Look for where small consistent inputs accumulate nonlinearly over time.
**Path Dependence** — Look for where current possibilities are constrained by historical decisions.
**Temporal Discounting** — Look for where short-term preference is destroying long-term value.
**Shifting Baseline Syndrome** — Look for how each generation accepts a degraded state as the new normal.

## 3. Boundaries, Scope & Framing

**Circle of Competence** — Look for whether you're inside or outside your actual zone of expertise.
**Map vs. Territory** — Look for the gap between your representation of reality and reality itself.
**Overton Window** — Look for what's currently unsayable that might actually be true or useful.
**Frame Control** — Look for who set the frame — and what becomes visible if you change it.
**Streetlight Effect** — Look for whether you're searching where it's easy rather than where the answer is.
**McNamara Fallacy** — Look for important factors not being measured because they're hard to quantify.
**Availability Heuristic** — Look for whether this seems important because it IS important or because it's memorable.
**Sunk Cost Awareness** — Look for where past investment is distorting current decision-making.

## 4. Incentives, Behavior & Agency

**Goodhart's Law** — Look for metrics that have become targets and stopped measuring what they were supposed to.
**Campbell's Law** — Look for where high-stakes quantitative indicators corrupt the processes they monitor. (Stronger Goodhart's.)
**Principal-Agent Problem** — Look for misalignment between who decides and who bears consequences.
**Moral Hazard** — Look for where insulation from consequences encourages risk-taking.
**Cobra Effect / Perverse Incentives** — Look for where an intervention creates the opposite of its intended outcome.
**Hanlon's Razor** — Look for whether stupidity or misaligned incentives explain this better than intentional harm.
**Skin in the Game** — Look for whether the recommender would do it with their own money/reputation/life. (Taleb)
**Revealed Preferences** — Look for what actions reveal about true priorities, regardless of stated intentions.
**Mimetic Desire** (Girard) — Look for where desire is imported from others rather than arising from genuine need.
**Status Games** — Look for where status competition is the hidden driver of seemingly irrational behavior.

## 5. Systems & Complexity

**Feedback Loops** — Look for self-reinforcing (positive) or self-correcting (negative) dynamics.
**Emergence** — Look for where the whole does something none of the parts can do alone.
**Butterfly Effect** — Look for where small changes in initial conditions produce vastly different outcomes.
**Leverage Points** (Meadows) — Look for where a small push would move the whole system.
**Chesterton's Fence** — Look for why this was built before deciding to tear it down.
**Iatrogenics** — Look for where intervention causes more harm than the original problem.
**Antifragility** (Taleb) — Look for where stressors make the system stronger rather than weaker.
**Gall's Law** — Complex systems that work evolved from simple systems that worked. Don't build complex from scratch.
**Homeostasis** — Look for where the system is actively fighting your intervention.
**Network Effects** — Look for where connections between nodes are more valuable than the nodes themselves.
**Phase Transitions** — Look for where the system is about to flip from one state to a fundamentally different one.

## 6. Information & Knowledge

**Asymmetric Information** — Look for who knows what — and what they're not telling you.
**Signal vs. Noise** — Look for whether this data point is real signal or random variation.
**Survivorship Bias** — Look for the invisible failures excluded from the data you're seeing.
**Selection Bias** — Look for whether the data is representative or systematically skewed.
**Base Rate Neglect** — Look for how often this type of thing actually happens before evaluating this specific case.
**Narrative Fallacy** (Taleb) — Look for where you're inventing a story to explain what might be coincidence.
**Confirmation Bias** — Look for whether you're searching for evidence or searching for confirmation.
**Berkson's Paradox** — Look for spurious correlations created by conditioning on a collider variable.
**Legibility** (James C. Scott) — Look for where the drive to make things readable/countable destroys important complexity.
**Curse of Knowledge** — Look for where your expertise blinds you to how others see this.

## 7. Strategy & Competition

**Comparative Advantage** — Look for where you're RELATIVELY better, not absolutely better.
**Moats** — Look for structural factors preventing competition from eroding your position.
**Red Queen Effect** — Look for where standing still means falling behind.
**Nash Equilibrium / MAD** — Look for stable states where no party can improve unilaterally.
**Asymmetric Warfare** — Look for where a smaller player can win by changing the rules.
**Winner's Curse** — Look for where winning means you've overpaid.
**Barriers to Exit** — Look for what keeps players in a game they should leave.
**Blue Ocean / Category Creation** — Look for uncontested spaces rather than competing in existing ones.

## 8. Human Judgment & Decision-Making

**Anchoring** — Look for how the first number or frame is distorting everything after.
**Loss Aversion** — Look for where fear of loss (felt 2x stronger) prevents pursuit of gains.
**Dunning-Kruger Effect** — Look for where confidence is highest and calibration is worst.
**Planning Fallacy** — Look for whether estimates use the inside view (hope) or outside view (how things usually go).
**Hindsight Bias** — Look for whether this was actually foreseeable or just seems that way now.
**Paradox of Choice** — Look for where reducing options would improve decisions.
**Peak-End Rule** — Look for the most intense moment and the final moment — those dominate the memory.
**Commitment and Consistency Bias** — Look for where past commitments override current evidence.
**Halo Effect** — Look for where excellence in one area is incorrectly assumed in another.

## 9. Change, Growth & Evolution

**Natural Selection** — Look for what's varying and what's selecting — that's where evolution is happening.
**Punctuated Equilibrium** — Look for whether you're in a stable period or on the edge of sudden shift.
**Creative Destruction** (Schumpeter) — Look for what this new thing will kill — and what the death creates space for.
**J-Curve** — Look for whether current deterioration is failure or the dip before improvement.
**Local vs. Global Optimum** — Look for whether you'd need to get worse before you could get much better.
**Adaptive Valley** — Look for the valley you'd have to cross to reach the higher peak.
**Jevons Paradox** — Look for where efficiency improvements increase total consumption rather than decrease it.
**Constructive Disruption** — Look for what needs to be broken to unlock the next level.

## 10. Value, Trade-Offs & Allocation

**Opportunity Cost** — Look for what you're giving up by choosing this option.
**Marginal Thinking** — Look for the value of the NEXT unit, not the average.
**Externalities** — Look for costs or benefits imposed on parties not in the transaction.
**Tragedy of the Commons** — Look for where individually rational behavior depletes a shared resource.
**Optionality** — Look for where preserving future flexibility is more valuable than committing now.
**Reversibility** — Look for whether this is a one-way door or two-way door. Calibrate caution accordingly.
**Zero-Sum vs. Positive-Sum** — Look for whether both parties can win or one's gain requires the other's loss.
**Asymmetric Upside / Convexity** — Look for where you risk little but stand to gain enormously.
