# Writing Type: Thought-Leadership Article

## What it is
A longer, more authoritative published piece that takes a defined position on a topic and argues it with evidence. Designed to build the writer's reputation as a thinker in their field. Higher stakes than a blog post — readers will judge the author's credibility on this.

## Default length
1,200–2,000 words.

## Structure
- Opens with a sharp framing of the problem or the conventional view being challenged
- States the thesis explicitly (no burying)
- Builds the argument with named evidence: studies, examples, named experts, primary sources
- Engages the strongest counter-argument honestly — does not dodge it
- Ends with the implication or call — what the reader should do or believe differently

## Voice
Authoritative but not academic. Confident, willing to take a position. First person used sparingly — the argument carries the weight, not the writer's persona. Hedging is allowed only when it's deliberate and explained.

## What success looks like
A peer in the field reads it and respects the thinking. The reader changes their mind on something — even slightly. Someone forwards it with the line "this is the best take I've seen on X."

## Watch out for
- Hedged claims that protect the writer instead of serving the reader
- Citing sources without verifying them
- Counter-evidence brushed aside instead of engaged
- "What do you think?" endings that abdicate the writer's position
- Inflated language that signals "important article" instead of being one

## How roles use this file
- **Strategist:** the thesis must be defensible AND non-obvious; if a smart person can't argue the opposite, it's too safe
- **Researcher:** sources are load-bearing here; verify everything; aim for primary sources where possible
- **Outliner:** classic structure: framing → thesis → evidence → counter-engagement → implication
- **Drafter:** authoritative register; cite specifically; acknowledge the trade-off when recommending
- **Skeptic:** strictest fact-check pass of any piece type — every concrete claim must verify
- **Line Editor:** every claim should trace to something the Researcher verified; cut hedges that aren't earning their keep
