# Writing Type: Newsletter

## What it is
A short, regular dispatch from the writer to a subscribed audience. Reads like a letter from someone who knows things and is sharing them with you specifically. More personal cadence, more direct address, less formal structure than a blog post.

## Default length
400–700 words.

## Structure
- Opens like a conversation, not an essay
- One observation, idea, or story — not a survey
- Often skips formal subheaders entirely
- Closes with a thought that lingers, not a summary

## Voice
Personal and direct. First person throughout. Reader is addressed as "you." Conversational rhythm — short sentences mixed with the occasional longer one. Comfortable being a little vulnerable.

## What success looks like
The reader feels they got something useful AND they feel like the writer is a real person they know. They open the next one when it arrives.

## Watch out for
- Sounding like a corporate broadcast
- Padding to hit a word count
- "Hope you had a great week!" filler
- Subheaders that fragment what should flow as a single voice
- Overformatting — bullet lists in a piece that should breathe as prose

## How roles use this file
- **Strategist:** the angle should feel personal — "here's what I've been thinking about" rather than "here are 5 strategies"
- **Outliner:** often only 2–3 unmarked sections; resist subheaders unless the piece truly needs them
- **Drafter:** prioritize voice and intimacy; one strong observation beats three weak ones
- **Line Editor:** preserve the conversational rhythm; cut anything that sounds broadcast-y
