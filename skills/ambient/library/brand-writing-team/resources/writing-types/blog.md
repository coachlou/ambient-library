# Writing Type: Blog Post

## What it is
A published piece on the writer's blog. One central argument or insight, supporting evidence, earned ending. More structured than a newsletter, less rigorous than a thought-leadership article.

## Default length
800–1,200 words.

## Structure
- Opener that earns attention in the first 100 words
- One central thesis (not a survey of three things)
- 2–4 supporting sections with subheaders
- Ending that lands somewhere — a turn or payoff, not a summary

## Voice
Friendly-authoritative. Conversational but confident. First person welcome. Can address the reader directly.

## What success looks like
The reader keeps scrolling, learns or reframes something specific, and could quote one line back to a friend.

## Watch out for
- Generic openers ("In today's fast-paced world…")
- Listicle padding (10 tips when 3 would do)
- "In conclusion" wrap-up paragraphs
- Tacked-on CTAs

## How roles use this file
- **Strategist:** prioritize one arguable thesis over a survey
- **Outliner:** 3–5 sections is the sweet spot; subheaders welcome
- **Drafter:** friendly-authoritative tone, lead with the answer, earned ending
- **Line Editor:** verify scannability and clean transitions
