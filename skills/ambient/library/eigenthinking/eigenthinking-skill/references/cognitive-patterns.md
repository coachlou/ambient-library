# Cognitive Patterns: Surfacing Expert Thinking

A reference for recognizing and drawing out the cognitive eigenvectors 
that are unique to each expert user. Use this during Steps 1–3 (SIGNAL, 
DRILL, REFRAME) to help users articulate what they do intuitively.

---

## What Cognitive Eigenvectors Look Like

Cognitive eigenvectors are the recurring patterns in how an expert thinks — 
the axes along which their thinking consistently produces disproportionate 
insight. They show up as:

- Questions they instinctively ask that others don't
- The first thing they notice in a new situation
- What they find obvious that peers find complex
- What bothers them about standard approaches in their field
- Where they consistently arrive at different (and better) diagnoses

---

## Common Eigenvector Types

### The Structural Finder
*Pattern:* Immediately looks for the mechanism underneath the symptom.  
*Signal phrase:* "That's not the real problem. The real problem is..."  
*Eigenthinking application:* Natural at SIGNAL and DRILL. 
Needs encouragement to slow down and articulate the drilling steps 
explicitly — they do it so fast they skip steps without noticing.

### The Abstraction Climber
*Pattern:* Compulsively moves to higher levels of abstraction.  
*Signal phrase:* "What does this generalize to?" or "This is an instance of..."  
*Eigenthinking application:* Natural at LIFT. 
May rush through BUILD before the methodology is solid enough to lift. 
Hold them at BUILD until the quality gate is passed.

### The Pattern Namer
*Pattern:* Immediately seeks to name and label phenomena.  
*Signal phrase:* "There should be a word for this."  
*Eigenthinking application:* Natural at NAME. 
Risk of premature naming — naming before the REFRAME is solid. 
Explicitly sequence NAME after REFRAME is confirmed.

### The Compression Artist
*Pattern:* Experiences long explanations as evidence of incomplete thinking.  
*Signal phrase:* "Can we make this shorter?" or "What's the one-line version?"  
*Eigenthinking application:* Natural at COMPRESS. 
May compress prematurely and lose mechanism. 
Ensure full BUILD is complete before running COMPRESS.

### The Friction Seeker
*Pattern:* Treats recurring irritants as the most reliable signal of important problems.  
*Signal phrase:* "This keeps bothering me and I can't figure out why."  
*Eigenthinking application:* Natural at SIGNAL. 
Exceptionally good at finding genuinely important problems. 
Needs structure to convert friction into diagnosis.

### The Democratizer
*Pattern:* Immediately asks how a discovery can be made available to others.  
*Signal phrase:* "How do I make this teachable?" or "Everyone should know this."  
*Eigenthinking application:* Natural at TEACH. 
Strong motivation for completing the full cycle. 
May skip STRESS TEST in the rush to publish — hold them there.

### The Systems Architect
*Pattern:* Sees connections between components before seeing the components individually.  
*Signal phrase:* "How does this fit with..." or "This changes everything downstream."  
*Eigenthinking application:* Natural at BUILD. 
Risk of over-engineering the methodology with too many components. 
Apply compression test early and often during BUILD.

## How to Surface Eigenvectors in Conversation

### The Recurrence Probe
> "What's something you keep noticing or doing differently from your peers, 
> even when you can't fully explain why?"

### The Expertise Frustration Probe
> "What's the thing in your field that everyone gets wrong that drives you crazy?"

### The Teaching Frustration Probe
> "What's the thing you try to teach clients that they just can't seem to learn 
> from what you say — they only learn it by watching you do it?"

### The Origin Story Probe
> "Tell me about a time when you solved something that stumped everyone else. 
> What did you see that they didn't?"

### The Effortless Excellence Probe
> "What's something you do in your work that feels obvious or effortless to you 
> but that clients or peers seem to find remarkable?"

These probes surface eigenvectors by targeting the gap between what the expert 
experiences as ordinary and what their audience experiences as exceptional.

## Accessing Non-Modal AI Output

Standard prompting produces modal output — the high-probability, conventional 
response from the center of the training distribution. Eigenthinking requires 
non-modal output: insight from the regions shaped by the user's specific expertise.

### Techniques for Non-Modal Access

**Structural Incompatibility Prompt**  
> "Generate four positions on this question that are structurally incompatible — 
> where if one is correct, the others cannot be. Positions that can be merged 
> into 'it depends' are not divergent enough."

**Shadow Harvest Prompt**  
> "What positions on this question are held by credible minorities but suppressed 
> in mainstream discourse? What does the contrarian who's actually right believe?"

**Adjacent Domain Import**  
> "How would a [geologist / mathematician / ecologist / military strategist / 
> architect] diagnose this problem? What concepts from their field apply here?"

**Boundary Probe**  
> "Push this position to its logical extreme. Where does it break? What's 
> on the other side of the break?"

**Faction Analysis**  
> "Whose worldview does each of these positions represent? What intellectual 
> tradition, professional background, or epistemic framework would produce each one?"

These techniques draw from the Latent Space Cartographer methodology — the same 
non-modal access principles applied to the knowledge work context.

## The Cognitive Fingerprint (Optional Deliverable)

For full Eigenthinking sessions, offer to produce a cognitive fingerprint for 
the user: a reverse-engineered profile of their thinking patterns, query habits, 
optimization priorities, and discovery sequence.

The cognitive fingerprint is produced by:
1. Observing the user's conversation patterns throughout the session
2. Identifying recurring patterns (see eigenvector types above)
3. Naming the discovery sequence they naturally follow
4. Articulating what they're optimizing for, in priority order
5. Describing the worldview underneath the patterns

This becomes IP itself — a named, documented description of the user's cognitive 
eigenvectors that can anchor a personal brand, inform teaching materials, and 
guide future Eigenthinking cycles.
